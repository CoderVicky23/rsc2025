Invitation

The Research Scholar's Conclave 2025 is set to take place at the National Institute of Technology Raipur on December 15th-17th, 2025, bringing together researchers, academicians, and experts from diverse fields. This year, the conclave is centered around the theme "Sustainable Technology," focusing on innovative research in Engineering, Basic Sciences, and Humanities. It aims to foster discussions on cutting-edge advancements that promote sustainability across multiple domains.

For the first time, the conclave is open to research scholars from reputed institutes across the country, encouraging healthy academic competition and collaboration. This expansion provides an excellent opportunity for scholars to present their research, engage in insightful discussions, and build meaningful professional networks. The event will feature technical sessions, panel discussions, and keynote addresses by distinguished experts, offering valuable exposure to emerging trends and developments.

A key highlight of the conclave is the Scopus-indexed proceedings, ensuring that the presented research gains global visibility and recognition. Participants will have the opportunity to contribute to high-impact discussions, receive constructive feedback, and explore potential collaborations that can drive their research forward.

With a focus on sustainability-driven innovations and interdisciplinary knowledge exchange, the Research Scholar’s Conclave 2025 promises to be an enriching experience for all attendees. We look forward to an engaging and successful event, bringing together some of the brightest minds working toward a sustainable future.

About the Event

The Office of Dean (R&C), NIT Raipur, is organizing a three-day Research Scholars' Conclave from 15th-17th Dec 2025 at the NIT Raipur campus in offline mode. Under the leadership of the Dean (R&C), this year's conclave is set to witness a qualitative enhancement with the introduction of Scopus-indexed proceedings, ensuring global visibility and recognition for the presented research.

For the first time, the conclave is open to research scholars from other reputed institutes, fostering healthy competition and collaborative research opportunities. The event serves as a multidisciplinary platform for scholars to showcase their research, exchange innovative ideas, and contribute to cutting-edge advancements in sustainable and transformative technologies.

Parallel sessions will be conducted across different research domains, with expert evaluations, and the best presentations will be recognized and awarded. The conclave will act as a catalyst for impactful research and academic excellence, driving the institution towards a future rooted in sustainability and innovation.

ManuScript Submission Guidelines

How to submit the ManuScript

  • Login to your RSC2025 MicroSoft CMT Account ➤ Submission ➤ Information ➤ Update File (Right Corner)

Guidelines for Preparing ManuScript

  • Original manuscript should be strictly structured in preferably up to 8-12 pages and formatted using Springer’s styles (available on MISP2022 Website)
  • Total similarity score (plagiarism %) must be less than or equal to 20%. Plagiarism % from a single source must be less than or equal to 2 %.
  • Sentence formation must be correct and advised to use some spell checker or Grammarly software during proofreading.
  • All figures, tables, equations, and references used in the paper must be cited in the text at proper places.
  • All references must be in the style as prescribed.
  • All figures must be of good quality (at least 300 dpi).
  • In the author section, all the authors must have affiliation information, corresponding authors must be marked with * and Email id of all the authors must be provided.
  • All the equations must be written in the equation editor (in Math type / Microsoft 3.0).

After a double-blind peer review, qualifying Regular Papers may be accepted as either Full Papers or Short Papers.

  • As per policy, RSC2025 proceedings contain original research that has neither been published nor submitted for publication elsewhere. It is necessary that neither plagiarism nor self-plagiarism has taken place. It is possible that your manuscript will be run through the plagiarism-checking tool, following the arrival of the files at Conference.
  • It must be noted that in case of plagiarism or non-implementation of reviewers comments in CRC, your paper may NOT be included in proceedings. (This decision is to be taken by the publisher. It will be final and binding)
  • For detailed instructions for author of conference proceedings, kindly visit the following link: To be updated soon....
  • No extra fee is charged from authors for inclusion in the Book Series.
  • Authors will grant a non-exclusive, revocable license that allows providing services to users.

National Institute of Technology Raipur

National Institute of Technology Raipur (Formerly Government Engineering College Raipur) spared over an area of 100 acres and located in Raipur, the Capital City of Chhattisgarh State. Raipur city is well connected with Mumbai, Delhi, Chennai, Visakhapatnam, Nagpur & Bhubneshwar by regular flights and is on the main Howrah-Mumbai railway line route. NIT Raipur has proven to be "avant-grade' in the field of science and technology over past few decades in this region. With sweet memory of foundation ceremony by our president Hon'ble Dr. Rajendra Prasad on 14th September 1956. the institute started with two departments namely Metallurgical and Mining Engineering. Later the inauguration of the Institute building was done by our Prime Minister Hon'ble Pt. Jawahar Lal Nehru on 14th March 1963. From 1st December 2005, the institute has become the National Institute of Technology.

Presently NIT Raipur offers 12 undergraduate, 11 Postgraduate (including M.C.A and M.Tech. in Applied Geology) and 18 Ph.D programs. The institute offers facilities for Ph.D. and also under takes R & D activities, provides testing, consultancy and other extension services including continuing education to the industry through the Industry Institute Interaction cell and the placement of the student through the Department of Training & Placement. The students have brought laurels to the institute and very well placed in Government organisation, Public and Private Sectors, Multinational Companies, Universities, research organisation in India and abroad.

About Raipur

Raipur is the capital city of Chhattisgarh state in central India. The city is located centrally in the state of Chhattisgar. Raipur's economy has been based on agricultural-processing and saw-milling, and now serves as a regional hub for trade and commerce for a variety of local agricultural and forest products. In the center, the ancient Dudhadhari Math temple is a sacred monument dedicated to Lord Rama, decorated with scenes from the epic poem “Ramayana.” Nearby, a statue of the Hindu monk Swami Vivekananda towers over Vivekananda Sarovar lake. Southeast, Purkhouti Muktangan is an open-air museum featuring landscaped grounds, statues and tribal artifacts.

About Chhattisgarh

Chhattisgarh is a heavily forested state known for its temples and waterfalls. Situated in the heart of India with a ten thousand years old civilization it is a land ‘Full of Surprises’ that attracts tourists who look forward to experience the pristine. Chhattisgarh is an uniquely Indian experience. Home to the country’s widest waterfalls, caves, lush green forests, ancient monuments, rare wildlife, exquisitely curved temples, Buddhist sites and hill plateaus. Chhattisgarh boasts of more than 80% of bio diversity that is not found anywhere across the country. For centuries its tribal communities have played a pivotal role in nurturing and protecting the natural habitat through eco-friendly practices. Chhattisgarh offers everything to a traveller - art and architecture, heritage, handicrafts, cuisine, fairs and festivals. Near the capital Raipur, the town of Sirpur on the Mahanadi River is home to the red-brick Lakshmana (Laxman) Temple, decorated with carvings from Indian mythology. Chhattisgarh figures in numerous tales that form part of the two great Indian epics, the Ramayana and the Mahabharata.
